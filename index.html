Key Themes Emerging

The Optimism-Preparation Gap

Teens are broadly optimistic about AI-era careers, but most lack the formal AI skills training employers now require — a dangerous disconnect that schools must urgently close

University AI Enrollment Surge

AI-related programs are seeing explosive demand nationwide. UB's 40% enrollment jump reflects a broader shift as students recognize AI fluency as a career essential

Learning Integrity in the AI Era

Harvard's research draws a critical line between AI-augmented learning (beneficial) and AI-substituted learning (harmful) — and calls for "AI-resilient" course design across higher education

Institutional-Scale AI Investment

Penn State's $50M commitment signals a new phase: universities are no longer piloting AI education but institutionalizing it — with mandatory courses, new faculty, and open-source curriculum

The PISA 2029 Countdown

With AI literacy joining PISA's global assessment in 2029, the US faces a reckoning: no national framework, inconsistent teacher training, and a ZIP-code-dependent patchwork of AI education

K-12 Policy Vacuum

68% of districts have no AI policy, and only 14 states have issued guidance. The absence of a national framework is widening the equity gap between well-resourced and under-resourced schools
