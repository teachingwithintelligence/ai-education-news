-5%

  • Wage growth in AI-exposed sectors outpaces national averages: computer systems design up 16.7% vs. 7.5% nationally

Key Themes Emerging

Cognitive Crisis in Classrooms

The Brookings Institution's landmark report warns AI is causing a "great unwiring" of students' brains — creating "cognitive debt" and a generation of learners in "passenger mode" who outsource thinking entirely to AI

AI Cheating Is Now the Norm

Pew Research finds 59% of teens believe AI cheating is a regular feature of school life. With 54% using AI for schoolwork, schools face mounting pressure to redesign assessments and update integrity policies

National-Scale Teacher AI Training

Google and ISTE+ASCD's three-year initiative to train all 6 million U.S. educators marks a shift from ad hoc AI adoption to structured, nationwide professional development grounded in pedagogy and ethics

Interdisciplinary AI Degrees Surge

UB's "AI+X" model — pairing AI with economics, policy, communication, and more — is drawing record enrollment and inspiring similar programs nationwide, reflecting growing demand for AI literacy across all fields

Entry-Level Job Market Disruption

Dallas Fed data confirms AI is replacing entry-level workers while boosting wages for experienced ones. Young graduates face a shrinking job ladder as firms find AI more cost-effective than hiring new employees for codifiable tasks

AI Policy & Curriculum Reform

From Stanford's AI+Education Summit to new degrees at Georgetown and Kennesaw State, institutions are racing to redesign curricula, craft AI policies, and develop "AI-resistant" assessments that preserve authentic learning
