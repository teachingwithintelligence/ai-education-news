Google for Education / ISTE+ASCD

Google Launches Landmark AI Literacy Training for All 6 Million U.S. Educators

6M
  • Google for Education announces the largest AI teacher training initiative of its kind — a partnership with ISTE+ASCD to provide free, comprehensive Gemini training to all 6 million K-12 and higher education faculty in the U.S.
  • Training covers 74 million students through their educators, with bite-sized, flexible modules designed for busy teachers — each earning micro-credential badges upon completion
  • Topics include creating personalized lessons from same-day assessment results, using Gemini as an individualized study coach, and adapting curriculum for diverse learning needs including reading levels and primary languages
  • Materials align with ISTE+ASCD's "AI Ready Graduate" framework — equipping students with essential AI competencies for future success
  • Comes as a House subcommittee hearing (Feb. 24) stressed that AI professional development is a national must, with bipartisan support for federal resources
  • Only 13% of districts have a clear AI policy for both students and teachers; 44% of teachers have received zero professional development on AI tools, per EdWeek Research Center
  • Initiative will roll out in coming months; educators can express interest via Google's registration form

Pew Research Center

Pew Research: 54% of U.S. Teens Use AI for Schoolwork — 59% Say AI Cheating Is Routine at Their School

54%
  • New Pew Research Center survey of 1,391 U.S. teens ages 13–17 finds 54% use AI chatbots to help with schoolwork — primarily for researching topics and solving math problems
  • 57% use chatbots to search for information; 47% use AI for fun or entertainment; about 1 in 5 use AI to get news
  • About a quarter of all teens say chatbots have been extremely or very helpful for completing schoolwork; just 3% say they were of little to no help
  • 59% of teens believe AI cheating is a regular occurrence at their school — including one-third who say it happens extremely or very often
  • Teens view AI's impact on their personal lives more positively (36%) than negatively (15%); but are more critical of AI's broader societal impact
  • Top teen concerns about AI: overreliance and loss of critical thinking (34%), job displacement (25%), and misinformation (10%)
  • Schools are actively racing to craft AI policies in response, with New York City announcing new guidelines for public school classrooms

Coursera / Business Wire

4 in 5 Students Say AI Improved Their Academic Performance — But Only 20% of Universities Have a Formal AI Policy

80%
  • New Coursera report based on 4,200+ university students and educators across the U.S., U.K., India, Mexico, and Saudi Arabia finds 80% of students say AI has positively supported their learning experience
  • 95% of students and educators report using AI tools in their academic work; 78% say AI is having a positive impact on higher education overall
  • 70% believe AI will improve exam performance and the overall quality of higher education in coming years
  • Yet only 20% of educators report that their university has a formal AI policy in place — a stark governance gap
  • Globally, 56% of students and educators believe their higher education system is unprepared to manage AI's impact
  • 63% of students say they use AI for less than half of their academic tasks; only 5% use AI for more than 80% of their work
  • Coursera Chief Content Officer: "AI is delivering real benefits on campus, yet many institutions are still working to keep pace" — calling for stronger governance, faculty training, and thoughtful implementation

Fortune

'Students Can't Reason': Teachers Warn AI Is Fueling a Crisis in Kids' Ability to Think

⚠️
  • "Students can't reason. They can't think. They can't solve problems," lamented one teacher in a new Brookings Institution report on AI and student cognitive development
  • Reading skills are particularly at risk — the capacity for "cognitive patience" (sustaining attention on complex ideas) is being diluted by AI's ability to summarize long-form text
  • Shift in student attitudes: "Teenagers used to say, 'I don't like to read.' Now it's, 'I can't read, it's too long'" — a fundamental change in cognitive tolerance
  • AI is producing a "homogeneity of ideas" in writing: each additional human essay contributed 2–8 times as many unique ideas as those produced by ChatGPT
  • One student summarized the allure simply: "It's easy. You don't need to use your brain" — a sentiment educators say is becoming dangerously normalized
  • More than 60% of K-12 teachers used AI-based tools in their classrooms in 2025, nearly double the share from the prior year, per EdWeek Research Center
  • Experts call for AI literacy curricula that emphasize critical evaluation of AI outputs, not just proficiency in using AI tools

Daily Nexus / UC Santa Barbara

UCSB College of Engineering to Offer New AI Major Starting Fall 2026 — With Required Ethics Course

BS
  • UC Santa Barbara's College of Engineering will offer a new Artificial Intelligence major to incoming computer science students starting fall 2026, becoming the second UC to offer an AI major after UC San Diego
  • Program includes interdisciplinary electives, existing CS courses, and six new AI-centric courses — including a required ethics course on AI's societal impacts
  • Department Chair emphasizes: "The ethics course is important because AI is going to have an immediate impact on the societal context, and it is important for people designing these systems to understand that impact"
  • Students will become proficient in building AI programs and Large Language Models (LLMs), with applications across CS, healthcare, and finance
  • Program requires a GPU cluster for new course instruction; university also expanding staff advisors to support the College of Engineering
  • Joins a wave of new AI undergraduate programs: Stevens Institute (BS in AI), Blinn College (AAS in AI), and others launching in 2026 as industry demand surges
  • The major will be available to all applicants for the 2027–28 academic year, with a phased rollout to incoming CS students first

Key Themes Emerging

Massive Push for Teacher AI Training

Google's landmark initiative to train all 6 million U.S. educators — combined with a bipartisan Congressional hearing stressing federal support for AI professional development — signals a major national pivot toward equipping teachers before students can be effectively guided in AI use.

Teen AI Adoption Outpaces Policy

Pew Research confirms 54% of U.S. teens use AI for schoolwork, yet 59% say AI cheating is routine at their school. Schools are racing to craft policies, but the governance gap remains wide — with only 20% of universities reporting a formal AI policy, per Coursera's new report.

Cognitive Erosion Crisis

Teachers are sounding alarms: "Students can't reason. They can't think." Fortune's reporting on the Brookings study reveals AI is diluting cognitive patience, homogenizing student writing, and creating a generation that finds sustained reading and independent problem-solving increasingly difficult.

Wave of New University AI Programs

UCSB, Stevens Institute, Blinn College, and others are launching AI majors and degrees in 2026, reflecting surging industry demand. Notably, UCSB's program mandates an ethics course — recognizing that technical AI proficiency must be paired with understanding of societal impacts.

Federal Policy Vacuum and Political Tension

Trump's State of the Union praised AI in K-12, but the elimination of the Education Department's Office of Educational Technology has left schools without federal guidance. Bipartisan concern about AI's mental health impacts on minors is creating legislative friction with the administration's pro-AI stance.

AI Literacy as a National Imperative

With 95% of university students and educators using AI tools but only 20% of institutions having formal policies, AI literacy is no longer optional. The convergence of Google's 6M-educator initiative, Congressional hearings, and new degree programs reflects a national recognition that structured AI education is now essential infrastructure.
