Fortune / Pew Research Center

Pew Research: 54% of Teens Use AI for Schoolwork — and Many Parents Don't Know It

54%
  • Pew Research Center survey of 1,458 U.S. teens (ages 13–17) finds 54% use AI to help with schoolwork; 57% use it to search for information
  • One in ten teens says they do all or most of their schoolwork with chatbot assistance
  • 64% of teens report using chatbots, but only 51% of parents believe their teen uses the technology — a significant "perception gap"
  • About 40% use AI to summarize articles, books, and videos; 35% use it to edit their own writing
  • 59% of teens believe AI cheating is a regular occurrence at their school; one-third say it happens extremely or very often
  • 12% of teens have used chatbots for emotional support or advice — raising new concerns about AI companionship among youth
  • Teens view AI's impact on their own lives more positively (36%) than negatively (15%), but are more critical of its societal effects

Fortune / Brookings Institution

Brookings Report: AI Is the 'Fast Food of Education' — Fueling a Crisis in Student Reasoning

⚠️
  • Brookings Institution's yearlong study draws on 400+ research papers, hundreds of interviews, and expert consultations — one of the most comprehensive AI-in-education assessments to date
  • Teachers report students "can't reason, can't think, can't solve problems" — AI's "frictionless" nature removes the productive struggle where learning occurs
  • Students are "cognitively offloading" tasks to AI, creating "cognitive debt" — measurable decline in critical thinking, reading stamina, and writing originality
  • Researchers describe a "passenger mode" phenomenon: students physically present in school but effectively disengaged from learning
  • AI is producing a "homogeneity of ideas" in writing — each additional human essay contributes 2–8x more unique ideas than ChatGPT-generated ones
  • Report warns of a "great unwiring" of cognitive capacity — the counterpart to Haidt's "great rewiring" caused by social media
  • Even high-achieving students are pressured to use AI because it "demonstrably" improves grades, creating a positive feedback loop toward dependency

Key Themes Emerging

Massive Teacher AI Training Push

Google's 6-million-educator initiative — the largest of its kind — signals a turning point in how AI tools reach classrooms. Combined with a $23M pledge from Anthropic, Microsoft, and OpenAI to train 400,000 teachers, the industry is racing to shape how educators adopt AI.

Congress Demands Federal AI Guardrails

A Congressional hearing on February 25 revealed a governance vacuum: teachers are navigating AI without clear federal direction, relying on fragmented advice from states, tech companies, and social media. Bipartisan pressure is growing for standardized federal guidance.

Cognitive Crisis in the Classroom

The Brookings Institution's landmark report warns AI is creating a "great unwiring" of student cognition — removing the productive struggle that drives learning and producing measurable declines in reasoning, reading stamina, and writing originality across all grade levels.

Teen AI Usage Outpaces Parental Awareness

Pew Research finds 64% of teens use chatbots, yet only 51% of parents are aware of it. The "perception gap" is widest for AI — far exceeding the gap for social media — as parents struggle to keep pace with a technology that is newer and less visible than apps like Instagram or TikTok.

Wave of New AI Degree Programs

Universities are racing to launch AI-specific degrees: FAU's new MBA in AI, UNT's undergraduate AI degree launching fall 2026, UCSB's new AI major, and surging enrollment at Buffalo all reflect employer demand for graduates with formal AI credentials across business, engineering, and the sciences.

AI Policy Contradictions at the Federal Level

Trump's State of the Union championed AI in K-12 while his administration simultaneously eliminated the Office of Educational Technology and cut hundreds of STEM grants. This contradiction — celebrating AI education while dismantling its infrastructure — is creating confusion and concern among educators nationwide.
