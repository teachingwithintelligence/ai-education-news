HEPI — Student Generative AI Survey 2026

AI Use Now "Near Universal" Among Undergraduates — 95% Use It, 94% Use It for Assessed Work, New HEPI Survey Finds

95%
  • The Higher Education Policy Institute's Student Generative AI Survey 2026 (HEPI Report 199), based on 1,054 full-time UK undergraduates surveyed in December 2025, finds that 95% of students report using AI in at least one way — with 94% saying they use generative AI to help with assessed work, a figure that has risen sharply from 3% in 2024 to 8% in 2025 to 12% in 2026 for students directly including AI-generated text in assessed work
  • Nearly two-thirds (65%) of students say assessment has changed significantly in response to AI — reflecting a rapid institutional pivot toward AI-integrated coursework, oral exams, and portfolio-based evaluation as traditional written assessments become harder to authenticate
  • Almost half (49%) of students believe AI has improved their student experience, particularly by saving time, improving understanding, and providing instant support — but a minority report that AI has worsened their experience, citing concerns about fairness, skill erosion, social isolation, and future employment prospects
  • Around 15% of students report using AI to support their wellbeing — using it for companionship, advice, or to alleviate loneliness — a finding that raises significant questions about the emotional and social dimensions of AI integration in campus life
  • While over two-thirds (68%) of students believe AI skills are essential to thrive in today's world, fewer than half (48%) feel their teaching staff are helping them develop these skills for their future careers — pointing to a critical gap between student expectations and institutional delivery
  • Only 36% of students feel encouraged by their institution to use AI, and only 38% say they are provided with AI tools — suggesting that while student adoption has raced ahead, institutional support, guidance, and governance frameworks remain significantly underdeveloped across higher education

Digital Education Council — Global AI Student Survey 2026

92% of Students Now Use AI — But 65% Fear It Makes Learning Too Shallow, New Global Survey Finds

92%
  • The Digital Education Council's AI in Higher Education Latin America Survey 2026 finds that AI use among students is nearly universal at 92% — a 6% increase from the 2024 Global AI Student Survey, where adoption stood at 86%
  • Despite near-universal adoption, 65% of students feel concerned that AI may make learning too shallow and discourage critical thinking and creativity — a striking tension between widespread use and deep ambivalence about its educational value
  • 67% of students report using AI daily or weekly, with ChatGPT emerging as the most widely used tool (88% of frequent AI users), alongside Google Gemini, NotebookLM, Grok, and Claude
  • 73% of students foresee themselves using AI in their future jobs — suggesting that AI use during studies is closely connected to how students expect to work, making AI literacy a career preparation issue as much as an academic one
  • 56% of students express concern about data privacy when using AI tools, and 56% worry about the fairness of AI in evaluating their work — pointing to governance gaps that institutions must urgently address
  • While nearly half of students (50%) support teacher use of AI to provide feedback on assignments, they remain cautious about AI being used to grade or evaluate their performance — suggesting a clear boundary students want institutions to respect

Education Week — Securly Analysis

Real-Time Data: 1 in 5 Student AI Interactions on School Technology Involve Problematic Behavior, New Analysis Reveals

20%
  • An analysis by Securly of nearly 1.2 million student AI interactions across more than 1,300 school districts (Dec. 1, 2025 – Feb. 20, 2026) finds that roughly 1 in 5 interactions involved cheating, self-harm, bullying, or other problematic behaviors — with 1 in 50 flagged as potential red flags for violence, cyberbullying, or self-harm
  • ChatGPT is by far the most popular AI tool among K-12 students, accounting for 42% of interactions on school technology, followed by Securly's AI Chat (28%), Google Gemini (21%), and embedded ed-tech AI tools including MagicSchool, SchoolAI, and BriskTeaching (9%)
  • Among the more than 24,000 queries flagged as potentially unsafe, some involved students asking AI to help draft messages about suicidal thoughts or searching for information about self-harm — cases where Securly unmasked student identities and alerted district officials
  • The data underscores the urgent need for school districts to move beyond simple AI filtering toward proactive monitoring systems that can identify students in distress and connect them with support before harm occurs
  • Separate surveys cited in the analysis show that nearly 90% of university students globally use AI to help with their education, with roughly a third using AI tools daily — and almost 75% reporting their AI usage has increased since 2024

Education Week — Bipartisan Policy Center Commission

Bipartisan Commission Demands Bold National Action to Prepare Students for AI-Fueled Workforce Disruption

🏛️
  • A new report from the Bipartisan Policy Center's workforce commission — co-chaired by former governors and including two former U.S. Secretaries of Education — calls for a White House talent advisory council to form a unified, cross-government strategy for training workers in the AI era
  • The commission recommends a federal "K-12 scorecard" mapping the gaps between states' definitions of academic proficiency and performance on the National Assessment of Educational Progress — calling on states to dramatically raise standards to align with AI-era workforce demands
  • States and districts must rethink high school entirely, providing programs that allow students to graduate with credentials and skills sought by future employers — with federal incentives for competency-based education and new secondary education models
  • The commission calls for a national "beating the odds" incentive program providing increased Title I funding to states that show accelerated growth for English learners, students with disabilities, and other populations on college and career readiness metrics
  • Former U.S. Secretary of Education Margaret Spellings: "There will never be a substitute for teaching students to read and be numerate at some level. Period. With or without AI" — framing literacy as the non-negotiable foundation for any AI-era education strategy
  • Former Republican Governor Bill Haslam: "No matter where you are on the economic spectrum, this discombobulation is going to affect you. The strategy should be national, but the tactics should be state and local" — acknowledging the bipartisan urgency of AI workforce preparation

Northwestern Engineering — New AI Major

Northwestern University Launches Undergraduate AI Major This Fall — With Required Ethics Course for All Students

🎓
  • Northwestern University's McCormick School of Engineering announced a new undergraduate AI major launching fall 2026, administered by the Department of Computer Science — joining Carnegie Mellon and MIT in expanding dedicated AI academic programs
  • The curriculum will cover machine learning, natural language processing, AI systems and infrastructure, data structures and algorithms, and the mathematical foundations of modern AI — with all students required to take a course on the societal impact of AI, including ethics, privacy, sustainability, and intellectual property
  • "We aim to prepare students for the future of a quickly changing and expanding field. This new major will span the breadth of AI, while also preparing students to deploy AI systems, build systems that interact with users, and consider their impact," said AI Major Program Director Sara Owsley Sood
  • Graduates with AI expertise are in high demand across tech companies, startups, healthcare, government, and nonprofits — with the program designed to bridge the gap between academic computer science and applied AI in real-world environments
  • The AI major is the first offering of McCormick's new bachelor of science in engineering degree; undergraduate students enrolled in any Northwestern school may elect to add AI as a second major while remaining in their current school
  • About 60 students had already filled out the interest form within hours of the announcement — signaling strong demand from students who see AI specialization as critical to their career prospects in a rapidly evolving job market

🏙️
  • New York City's Department of Education is proposing the Next Generation Technology High School in Lower Manhattan — a selective school designed to prepare students for careers in AI, cybersecurity, computer science, and robotics, with Google and OpenAI "at the table" for curriculum partnerships

70%

Science|Business — Max Planck Society

Max Planck Society Launches Germany's First Cross-University Graduate School for Biomedical AI — PhD Applications Open September 2026

🧬
  • The Max Planck Society has selected the Max Planck School of Biomedical Artificial Intelligence as its fourth Max Planck School — the first initiative to systematically bring together universities and non-university institutes across Germany in a graduate programme focused on AI
  • Max Planck Society President Patrick Cramer: "The chosen subject focus addresses a particular challenge: the interface between biomedicine and artificial intelligence. This holds enormous potential for better understanding the causes of health and disease and for generating innovations in diagnostics and therapy"
  • The new School will join three existing Max Planck Schools (Cognition, Matter to Life, and Photonics) and aims to launch its first call for PhD applications on September 1, 2026 — funded through the Max Planck Society and the Dieter Schwarz Foundation
  • External reviewers included leading AI researchers from University College London, the University of Oxford's DeepMind Chair, and the Universidad Politécnica de Madrid — reflecting the international caliber of the programme's scientific advisory structure
  • The initiative reflects a broader global trend: as AI reshapes medicine and life sciences, universities are creating dedicated interdisciplinary graduate programmes that bridge technical AI expertise with domain knowledge in health, biology, and clinical research
  • The selection committee praised the concept's "clear scientific focus and coherent approach to the qualification of doctoral candidates" — noting its credible plans for infrastructure, computing resources, supervision model, curriculum, and an internationally visible network of fellows

Key Themes Emerging

Student AI Adoption Has Outpaced Institutional Readiness

Both the HEPI and Digital Education Council surveys released this week paint the same picture: student AI adoption is essentially universal, but institutional support, guidance, and governance frameworks remain critically underdeveloped. With 95% of UK undergraduates using AI and only 36% feeling encouraged by their institution to do so, higher education is facing a governance gap of historic proportions. The 12% of students now directly including AI-generated text in assessed work — up from 3% in 2024 — signals that the window for proactive policy development is closing rapidly.

K-12 AI Policy Is Bifurcating Between Innovation and Restriction

The contrast between New York City's proposal to open the nation's first AI-focused public high school and the five Community Education Councils calling for a two-year moratorium on AI in schools illustrates a deepening bifurcation in K-12 AI policy. Some jurisdictions are racing to build AI-native learning environments with Google and OpenAI as curriculum partners; others are legislating restrictions while they figure out the risks. The Bipartisan Policy Center commission's call for a federal K-12 scorecard and national standards suggests that this patchwork of local responses is inadequate for the scale of disruption AI is already causing.

Universities Are Racing to Launch Dedicated AI Programs

Northwestern's new AI undergraduate major — joining Carnegie Mellon, MIT, and others — reflects a broader wave of universities creating dedicated AI degree programs rather than simply adding AI modules to existing curricula. The Max Planck Society's new School of Biomedical AI in Germany signals that this trend is global, with institutions recognizing that interdisciplinary AI expertise requires purpose-built programs. Northwestern's decision to require an ethics course for all AI majors reflects a growing consensus that technical AI education without ethical grounding is insufficient preparation for the real-world deployment of these systems.

AI Safety Monitoring in Schools Is Becoming a Critical Infrastructure Need

The Securly analysis revealing that 1 in 5 student AI interactions on school technology involves problematic behavior — and that 1 in 50 raises red flags for violence or self-harm — reframes AI monitoring from a compliance issue to a student safety imperative. With students using AI to draft messages about suicidal thoughts and search for self-harm information, schools need monitoring infrastructure that can identify students in crisis and connect them with support. The data also highlights that ChatGPT dominates school AI usage at 42% of interactions, raising questions about whether districts have adequate visibility into what their students are actually doing with these tools.

The Graduate Job Market Is Facing an AI Reckoning

From ServiceNow CEO Bill McDermott's warning that AI agents could push college graduate unemployment into the mid-30s, to Stanford economists examining AI's reshaping of the labor market, to CS students expressing uncertainty about their career paths — the signals are converging. The Bipartisan Policy Center commission's call for bold national action frames this not as a distant risk but as a present disruption requiring immediate policy response. The 70% of Gen Z students who remain confident about their job prospects may be underestimating the pace of change, while those developing AI fluency early are already seeing concrete career advantages.

AI Wellbeing and Critical Thinking Concerns Are Reshaping Pedagogy

The HEPI survey's finding that 15% of students are using AI for companionship and to alleviate loneliness — alongside the 65% who fear AI makes learning too shallow — points to a dual challenge for educators: AI is simultaneously a cognitive shortcut that may undermine deep learning and a social substitute that may erode human connection. Professors at The Guardian's reported on academics scrambling to preserve critical thinking, while the Washington Post warned that schools are teaching AI use without teaching students to have agency over the technology. The pedagogical question of how to integrate AI while preserving the cognitive and social development that education is meant to provide has become the defining challenge of 2026.
