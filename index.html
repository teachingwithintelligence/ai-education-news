The 74 / Jeff Riley, Former State K-12 Commissioner

America Is About to Be Graded on AI Literacy. We Are Not Prepared.

🌐
  • In 2029, PISA will formally test AI literacy alongside reading, math, and science for the first time globally
  • US students currently rank ~28th in math, 6th in reading, 10th in science — and have no national AI literacy plan
  • A student's AI education depends entirely on their ZIP code, school district, and whether teachers are trained
  • "People with AI skills will replace those without AI skills" — industries will shift regardless of readiness
  • Call for a national AI literacy framework to ensure every student learns fundamentals before 2029
  • AI literacy is both an economic competitiveness issue and a matter of educational equity

Education Week

ETS Releases First AI Skills Assessment for Teachers

📋
  • ETS launched Futurenav Adapt AI — first standardized test measuring teacher AI competency
  • Three modules: understanding AI, navigating ethically, evaluating tools, and using AI in lessons
  • Scenarios include managing student data in AI tools and building lesson plans with a chatbot
  • Final section uses an AI-powered virtual instructional coach to assess and reflect on teaching
  • Takes under 30 minutes; designed to help district leaders identify professional development gaps
  • Most teachers currently receive limited or no formal training on AI before using it in classrooms

Harvard Gazette

Harvard Study: Nearly Half of Students Say They Rely on AI Too Much

🧠
  • Harvard survey of 7,000 high school students: nearly 50% say they rely on AI too much for learning
  • Over 40% of students tried to limit their AI usage but said it was too difficult and they failed
  • Students' motivation in math and English dropped significantly after AI was introduced
  • Harvard cognitive scientist Tina Grotzer: AI may impair critical thinking and cognitive development
  • Self-regulation identified as the key skill students need to use AI effectively without shortcutting learning
  • Researchers call for a fundamental rethinking of education's purpose in the AI era

🔍
  • LightLeap AI identified over 4,000 fraudulent spring 2026 applications at El Camino College

Key Themes Emerging

Global AI Literacy Race

With PISA adding AI literacy in 2029, the US faces a reckoning: no national framework, no consistent training, and a patchwork system that leaves millions of students behind

University AI Degree Surge

UCSB joins USC, Columbia, UT Austin, UB, Kennesaw State, and others in launching dedicated AI undergraduate programs for the 2026–2027 academic year

Career Anxiety & Major Shifts

AI is reshaping career choices before automation fully arrives — students are switching from CS to nursing and trades, while workers pivot to roles less exposed to AI displacement

Teacher AI Assessment Gap

ETS's new Futurenav Adapt AI test reveals a critical gap: most teachers use AI in classrooms without formal training, and districts lack tools to measure or address this readiness gap

Learning Erosion Concerns

Harvard research shows nearly half of students admit over-relying on AI, with declining motivation in core subjects — raising urgent questions about AI's impact on cognitive development

AI as Campus Security Tool

Beyond the classroom, AI is being deployed to protect institutional integrity — 116 California colleges now use AI to detect enrollment fraud, recovering millions in misallocated state funding
