College Board: 92% of Faculty Concerned AI Is Enabling Plagiarism in Higher Ed

92%
  • Survey of 3,000+ U.S. college faculty finds near-universal concern about AI's impact on academic integrity
  • 74% report students using AI to write essays; 67% say students use it to paraphrase or rewrite content
  • 84% agree AI reduces students' critical thinking, originality, and deep engagement with course material
  • 88% concerned about student overreliance on AI automation
  • Faculty sentiment skews negative: 45% overall negative view vs. 34% positive — yet 77% use AI in their own professional work
  • Only 21% feel confident guiding AI use in their classrooms; 79% still need guidance
  • STEM and business faculty more positive; English and humanities faculty most alarmed

Education Perfect / Angus Reid

77% of Teachers Stressed by AI Rollout — And It's Not a Generational Problem

77%
  • New Angus Reid survey of 300+ Canadian K-12 educators finds overwhelming majority stressed by pace of AI adoption
  • 65% believe their district is not adapting fast enough to provide safe, curriculum-aligned AI tools
  • AI stress is not generational: 71% of teachers aged 18–34 stressed; 76% of those aged 35–54
  • 64% say a single, unified AI-enabled platform would significantly reduce stress and workload
  • Report identifies a "Paradox of Pace": teachers are willing to adopt AI but overwhelmed by fragmented, rapidly changing tools
  • Governance gap: teachers across the country waiting for institutional clarity on which tools are safe and private

Dartmouth Went All-In on AI — Then Faculty Found Out the Company Stole Their Books

⚖️
  • Dartmouth College — birthplace of AI (1956 Dartmouth Conference) — signed a 2-year deal with Anthropic for 7,300 "Claude for Education" licenses
  • Faculty mobilized against the partnership after learning Anthropic settled a copyright lawsuit for training on 500,000 books
  • ~130 Dartmouth faculty and staff — including the college president — were claimants in the Anthropic lawsuit
  • Majority of faculty still ban generative AI in syllabuses; over half hadn't changed assessments to reflect AI as of last summer
  • Students divided: engineering students see AI as an asset; humanities students worry it limits their ability to learn
  • Provost called the deal a "bargain" at a faculty meeting, citing steep educational discount and pressure from limited federal research funding

Indiana University / NSF

NSF Funds "PrimaryAI" to Bring AI Literacy to Rural Elementary Classrooms in 3 States

K–5
  • National Science Foundation funds a 4-year project to introduce AI concepts to grades 3–5 in rural Indiana, Alabama, and North Carolina
  • Led by IU School of Education researchers Anne Leftwich and Cindy Hmelo-Silver using immersive problem-based learning
  • Addresses a critical gap: most AI education research focuses on secondary and higher ed, leaving elementary students behind
  • Research questions: What AI concepts work as entry points for rural teachers? How do local rural factors shape implementation?
  • Will measure impacts on student AI knowledge, STEM interest, and engagement across diverse rural contexts
  • Part of a broader national push to ensure AI literacy reaches underserved and rural communities, not just urban tech hubs

Key Themes Emerging

AI Enters the Political Spotlight

Trump's State of the Union highlighted AI in K-12 education for the first time, showcasing a 10-year-old AI advocate as Melania's guest — even as the administration has cut STEM grants and eliminated the Office of Educational Technology

Faculty Alarm Reaches a Tipping Point

College Board's survey of 3,000+ faculty reveals near-universal concern: 92% worry about AI-enabled plagiarism, 84% say AI is eroding critical thinking, yet 77% use AI themselves — creating a complex double standard in higher education

Teacher Wellbeing Under Pressure

The "Paradox of Pace" — teachers willing to adopt AI but overwhelmed by fragmented tools and absent governance — is emerging as a workforce crisis. Stress is uniform across age groups, signaling a systemic problem, not a generational one

AI Reshaping College Admissions

Nearly half of high schoolers now use AI in the college search, with AI influencing which schools they apply to and even whether they go to college at all. Institutions must rethink recruitment, communications, and how they appear in AI-generated answers

University AI Ethics Under Fire

Dartmouth's Anthropic partnership — where faculty whose books were allegedly stolen are now required to use the company's AI — captures the ethical minefield universities face when pursuing AI deals without adequate faculty consultation or transparency

Equity & Rural AI Access

NSF's PrimaryAI project and the community college AI consortium signal a growing push to extend AI literacy beyond elite urban institutions to rural elementary schools and community colleges — addressing a deepening digital divide in AI education
